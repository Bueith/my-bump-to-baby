Privacy Policy

Effective 25 June 2026 · Nurture by eCape Dev Studio

This policy explains what data Nurture collects, why we collect it, where it goes, and what control you have over it. We've kept this in plain language on purpose — if anything is unclear, email us at etudetc@gmail.com.

Who runs Nurture

Nurture is built and operated by eCape Dev Studio. Contact for any privacy question, data request, or complaint: etudetc@gmail.com.

What we collect

Only the information you give us directly:

  • Your name — entered during onboarding so the app can greet you.
  • Your stage and week — pregnancy or postpartum, used to tailor content.
  • Your daily logs — mood, sleep, energy, kicks, feeds, nappy counts, symptoms, and any notes you write.
  • Your Village entries — names, roles, and phone numbers of people you add yourself.
  • Your Care entries — appointments, emergency contacts, and private diary entries.
  • A web access code and web password — needed to log in on the website. The password is stored only as a one-way hash; it cannot be recovered or read by us.
  • Crash reports — when the app crashes, Firebase Crashlytics sends us a technical report (device model, Android version, where the crash happened). No personal content is included.

We do not collect: your location, your contacts, your photos, your browsing history, your microphone audio (voice input is processed by Android on-device and only the resulting text reaches us), advertising identifiers, or any data from third-party trackers. There are no ads in Nurture.

Where your data is stored

Two places, both yours:

  • On your phone — in encrypted local storage that only Nurture can read.
  • In Firebase Firestore (Google Cloud, europe-west1) — so the data also appears when you log into Nurture on the website. Each user's data is in a separate document protected by security rules that only your device can read or write.

We do not sell, share, or rent your data to any third party. We do not use it for advertising. We do not use it to train AI models.

Voice input

Where the app offers voice input (e.g. the daily log or Companion search), it uses Android's built-in speech recognition. The audio is processed by your device or by Google's speech service on your behalf — Nurture never receives the audio itself, only the recognised text once you finish speaking. You can use the keyboard instead at any time.

The Companion (AI Q&A)

When you ask a question in the Companion, Nurture first tries to match it against a small, hand-written set of answers stored in the app. If nothing matches, your question is sent to a search service (Serper.dev) which queries trusted medical sources (NHS, ACOG, Mayo Clinic and similar). Your name and other personal data are never sent with the question — only the question text itself.

Children

Nurture is for adults. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 18. If you believe a child has used the app, email us and we'll delete the account immediately.

Your rights

At any time you can:

  • Export your data — Settings → Export My Data gives you a JSON file with everything you've entered. We never receive a copy.
  • Reset everything on your phone — Settings → Reset App Data wipes the local copy.
  • Request deletion of your cloud data — email etudetc@gmail.com with your access code (the NURTURE-XXXXX code shown in Settings) and we'll delete the Firestore record within 7 days.
  • Ask what we hold about you — same email, we'll respond within 30 days.

Security

We use industry-standard practices: passwords are hashed with scrypt and a per-password salt, sessions are signed with HMAC and expire after 12 hours, the website rate-limits password attempts to protect against brute-forcing, and Firestore security rules ensure your document can only be read or written by your own device. We cannot promise perfect security — no service can — but we'll tell you within 72 hours if we ever learn of a breach affecting your data.

Changes to this policy

If we ever change what we collect or how we use it, we'll update this page and bump the effective date at the top. For material changes, we'll also notify users in-app before the change takes effect.
